## Summary / Ringkasan

Q1 actuals show **two material variances** against budget that the leadership team should be aware of for Q2 planning.

Q1 menunjukkan **dua varian material** terhadap anggaran yang perlu diperhatikan tim kepemimpinan untuk perencanaan Q2.

## 1. Consultant Spend (PT Pelangi Konsultindo)

The Pelangi advisory engagement (contract PLG/CONS/2026/047, effective 1 Maret 2026) was budgeted at USD 45,000 for Q1 but actuals came in at USD 58,200 — a **29% overrun**.

Root cause: scope expansion in the SEA architecture assessment after the Jakarta workshop in week 4. Pelangi's advisory rate of USD 185/hour is at the cap defined in Acme HR Policy HR-POL-2026-001 (Section 4); no rate variance, purely a volume variance.

Recommendation / Rekomendasi: do **not** request a rate concession from Pelangi (we would set a bad precedent and they are operating at policy cap). Instead, formalize scope-change protocol going forward — every new workstream over USD 5,000 needs written acknowledgement from the Acme VP of Engineering before work proceeds.

We also flag the **6-month auto-conversion-to-fixed-fee clause** (HR-POL-2026-001 Section 4; Kontrak Pelangi Pasal 3): if the engagement runs through September 2026, we automatically transition to fixed-fee. Finance should model both scenarios for Q3 planning.

## 2. Parental Leave Coverage

Three engineers in the Singapore office initiated 16-week parental leave during Q1 (per Acme HR Policy HR-POL-2026-001 Section 1). Two were birth parents, one was a non-birth parent — leave policy applies equally per the policy text.

Coverage cost (contractor backfill) is USD 38,500 against a Q1 contingency budget of USD 25,000 — a **54% overrun** on the contingency line, though net P&L impact is offset by lower salary expense during the leave windows.

Recommendation / Rekomendasi: increase Q2 contingency line to USD 40,000 reflecting normalized parental leave frequency observed over the last 4 quarters.

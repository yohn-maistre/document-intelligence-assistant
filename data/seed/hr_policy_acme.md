# Acme Corp HR Policy — 2026 Edition

**Document ID**: HR-POL-2026-001
**Effective date**: 2026-01-01
**Owner**: People Operations
**Review cycle**: Annual

## 1. Parental Leave

All full-time Acme Corp employees who have completed at least 90 days of continuous service are entitled to **16 weeks of paid parental leave** per qualifying event. This applies equally to birth parents, non-birth parents, and adoptive parents.

Leave may be taken as a single continuous block within 12 months of the qualifying event, or split into two blocks of at least 4 weeks each with manager approval.

Pay during parental leave is at 100% of base salary for the first 12 weeks and 60% for the remaining 4 weeks.

## 2. Annual Leave

Full-time employees accrue 25 working days of annual leave per calendar year, prorated by start date for partial years. A maximum of 10 unused days may be carried forward to the following year; unused balance beyond 10 days is forfeited on 31 March.

## 3. Sick Leave

Employees may take up to 10 paid sick days per year. Sick leave beyond 10 days requires a medical certificate and is paid at 80% of base salary for up to an additional 20 days.

## 4. External Consultants and Contractors

When Acme engages external consultants, the standard billing rate cap is **USD 185 per hour** for advisory work and **USD 145 per hour** for implementation work. Rates above the cap require VP-level approval and a written justification recorded in the procurement file.

Consultant engagements longer than 6 months automatically transition to a fixed-fee arrangement to control cost exposure.

## 5. Remote Work

Eligible roles may work remotely up to 4 days per week. The fifth day must be in-office unless an explicit waiver is granted by the line manager.

## 6. Expense Reimbursement

Reimbursable expenses include travel (economy class within 6 hours of flight time, business class beyond), meals (up to USD 75 per day), and approved professional development (up to USD 2,000 per year).

## 7. Anti-Discrimination

Acme Corp is committed to a workplace free of discrimination. All hiring, promotion, and disciplinary decisions are made without regard to protected characteristics as defined in applicable law.
